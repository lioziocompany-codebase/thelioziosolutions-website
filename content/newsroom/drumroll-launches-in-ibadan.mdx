LIOZIO Solutions announces the public launch of Drumroll, its flagship e-commerce and social commerce solution, in Ibadan, Nigeria. The platform introduces a pickup-first model that eliminates delivery complexity while giving urban households access to quality products at below-market prices.

Rather than compete with delivery-based e-commerce on its own terms, Drumroll was built around a different premise: that Nigerian urban consumers would embrace a pickup-first model when it was built with genuine quality and priced fairly. Customers order through the Drumroll website, WhatsApp, and social media channels, then collect their purchases from a designated pickup location — no delivery windows, no missed handoffs, no added logistics cost passed on to the customer.

The platform launches with three sub-brands already in place: Goodfood for foods and consumables, Bountiful for lifestyle and personal care, and Joombow for electronics and appliances. All three source directly from manufacturers and producers, removing the layers of middlemen that typically inflate prices in the Nigerian urban consumer market, and passing that saving on to the customer.

Drumroll's launch in Ibadan is the first commercial proof point for LIOZIO Solutions' broader thesis: that technology-powered, data-driven commerce can outcompete legacy retail at every level. With the model now live, the company's stated roadmap moves next to Lagos, with further expansion following confirmation of product-market fit.
